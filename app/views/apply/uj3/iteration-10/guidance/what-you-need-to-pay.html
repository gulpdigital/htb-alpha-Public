What you need to pay

There are 3 types of payment you need to make:

  • monthly £1 management fee
  • monthly interest fee (this starts after 5 years)
  • paying off the equity loan

Monthly management fee

You'll be charged a monthly £1 management fee, which is paid by direct debit from the start of owning your home until the loan is repaid.

Monthly interest fee

Interest payments start after 5 years at 1.75%. It then increases each year by a fixed formula which uses a measure of inflation called the Consumer Price Index plus Housing (CPIH).

The formula is the previous year's interest multiplied by (CPIH+2)%.

From 1 April of the 6th year after you bought your home onwards, each year the interest rate from the previous year is multiplied by (CPIH + 2)% and added to itself.

This formula is repeated until you repay the loan in full.

Paying off the equity loan

The monthly payments do not go towards repaying the equity loan.

You must repay the equity loan in full if you sell your home or pay off your mortgage. Or you can pay lump sums of 10% or 20% (or 30% in London) of the value of your home any time before then, depending on the percentage you borrowed.

The amount you repay will depend on the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors (RICS) property valuation of your home. You will need to have your home valued each time you make a repayment.

To repay your equity loan without moving home you could raise funds by remortgaging or use savings.

Repayment fees

You will need to apply for a repayment statement to make a repayment. This includes a £200 Help to Buy administration fee each time you apply. There will also be other fees such as for a solicitor and surveyor.

Example of repayment

This example shows how much you would repay if the market value of your home changes.

It is based on:

  • a property bought for £200,000
  • an original equity loan value of £40,000
  • an equity loan of 20%

You can repay a 20% equity loan in 1 or 2 lump sum repayments. The first repayment must be at least 10%, but you can’t leave less than 5% of the value remaining.
