How it works

An equity loan is used together with your mortgage and deposit to make up the property purchase price.

How much you can borrow to buy your new home depends on the region you plan to live in.

To buy a home with an equity loan, you need:

  • a deposit of at least 5% of the property price
  • a mortgage of at least 25% of the property price

If you have a large deposit and can secure a mortgage without assistance, consider if a Help to Buy: Equity Loan is the right option for you.

England (except London)

When buying a property in England:

  • your mortgage and deposit must add up to at least 80% of the property price
  • you can borrow an equity loan of between 5% and 20%

London

When buying a property in London:

  • your mortgage and deposit must add up to at least 60% of the property price
  • you can borrow an equity loan of between 5% and 40%

Examples

Examples

  • England (except London)
  • London borough

This example shows the minimum deposit percentage amount and maximum equity loan percentage amounts to buy a home in England, outside of London. This is based on a property price of £400,000.

Percentage Amount
Deposit 5% £20,000
Mortgage 75% £300,000
Equity loan 20% £80,000
Property price 100% £400,000

This example shows the minimum deposit percentage amount and maximum equity loan percentage amounts to buy a home in a London borough. This is based on a property price of £400,000.

Percentage Amount
Deposit 5% £20,000
Mortgage 55% £220,000
Equity loan 40% £160,000
Property price 100% £400,000

Property price limits

There is a price limit on the home that you buy. The limit is different for each region in England.

Region Full property price limit
East £407,400
East Midlands £261,900
London £600,000
North East £186,100
North West £224,400
South East £437,600
South West £349,000
West Midlands £255,600
Yorkshire and The Humber £228,100

If you have a Help to Buy ISA

If you have a Help to Buy ISA, you can use it to buy any property worth up to £250,000 (or up to £450,000 in London). You must be an existing holder to use a Help to Buy ISA because it closed in November 2019 and is no longer accepting new applications.
