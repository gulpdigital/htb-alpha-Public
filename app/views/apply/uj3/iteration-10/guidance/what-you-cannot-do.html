Restrictions

Owning a second home

You can't buy or own a second home. Your Help to Buy home must be the only home you own. You must pay back the equity loan before any other major expenditure such as buying another property.

Renting out your home

You can't rent out your home without written permission from the equity loan administrator. This will only be agreed in exceptional circumstances, for example if you are a member of the armed forces.

Renting out a room in your home is allowed while you continue to live there.

Making structural alterations

If you make structural changes which increase the value of your home, the amount you need to repay will increase.

If you have a medical or accessibility need to make structural changes, the resulting value increase can be excluded from how much you need to repay. To do this, you must get written permission from the loan administrator before you make the changes.
