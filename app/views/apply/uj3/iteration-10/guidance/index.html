Overview

With a Help to Buy: Equity Loan you can get help from the government to buy your first home.

What an equity loan is

A Help to Buy: Equity Loan allows first-time homebuyers to borrow a percentage of the property’s price. This reduces how much deposit you need.

Monthly interest payments start 5 years after you buy your home. There is also a £1 monthly management fee you need to pay as soon you buy.

Your equity loan can not be paid back in monthly instalments.

The loan must be repaid in full when you sell the property, pay off your mortgage in full, or reach the end of your mortgage term - whichever is soonest.

When repaying an equity loan, you get your home revalued and pay back the percentage of the property’s value that you borrowed.

The property you can buy

With an equity loan, the home you buy must be:

  • a new build. This is a newly built home that has not been lived in before
  • from a registered Help to Buy house builder

Homebuyers can use an equity loan to buy a home in England (there are different schemes for Wales and Scotland).

An equity loan is different to the shared ownership scheme.
