How to apply

Follow these steps to buy a home with an equity loan.

  • Before applying:

    1. Get a realistic idea of your mortgage amount
      You shouldn’t incur the costs of securing a mortgage at this stage, but you need to have a good idea of what you can borrow. An independent financial advisor can help you with this.
    2. Find and reserve your new home
      Find a new build home (a home which has not been lived in before) through your regional Help to Buy agent or from a registered homebuilder. Homebuilders can ask for a reservation fee of up to £500 which is refundable if your application is not approved.
    3. Find a conveyancing solicitor
      You will need to provide your solicitor's details when you apply for the equity loan.

  • Apply:

    1. Apply
      Apply online for a Help to Buy: Equity Loan. A decision will usually take around a week. A Help to Buy agent will get in touch with you to let you know if your application has been approved.

  • After your loan has been approved:

    1. Apply for your mortgage
      You will need to pay for a valuation of your new home to secure your mortgage agreement.
    2. Exchange and completion
      Your solicitor will advise you to check the details and will organise your deposit, mortgage and equity loan to be paid to the homebuilder, and for the details to be registered with Land Registry. Your name will be on the property deeds and Homes England will have a legal charge.
What if I'm not approved for the equity loan?

If your application is not approved, your reservation fee will be refunded.

You should get a realistic idea of mortgage options before you apply. You can do this without the expense of a valuation and survey fees.

Starting your application

Before applying, you must have already reserved a property and have a realistic idea of your mortgage.

You will need the following to complete the application:

  • new build property details
  • monthly income and outgoings
  • solicitor details
  • details to verify your identity

If you're buying with someone else, you'll need their income and outgoings too.

You should complete the service yourself unless you need assistance using digital services. Your financial advisor or homebuilder should not apply on your behalf.
