Show an example if my property price changes

For example, if your property price increases by 10%, the property would be worth £217,800.

With an 18% equity loan, you would repay 18% of the new property price.

As 18% of £217,800 is £39,204, this is how much equity loan you would repay excluding fees.

If the property's value decreases by 10%, it would be worth £178,200. This means you would repay £32,076, in equity loan excluding fees.

This differs from a normal mortgage, which is does not change with property price.

Fees and interest

You must set up a direct debit of £1 per month for the first 5 years.

On the sixth year the fees will be 1.75% of the purchase price and then the fees will increase by RPI plus 1% each year.

If RPI is 2% for the year, the fees would go up by 3% of 1.75 (0.525) to make a total of 1.825%.
