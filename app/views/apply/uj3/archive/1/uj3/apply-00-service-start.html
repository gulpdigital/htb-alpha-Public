Apply for a Help to Buy: Equity Loan

Apply for a low-interest loan towards a deposit for a home.

You can get help from the government by applying for a Help To Buy: Equity Loan.

Who can apply

Homebuyers based in England can apply for this scheme. There are different schemes for homebuyers in Scotland and Wales.

Check you meet the conditions to apply for an equity loan.

Eligibility

You must be:

  • 18 or over
  • able to prove you can afford the mortgage repayments

The home you buy must:

  • be a new build
  • be the first property you own (if you’re applying in England)
  • not be sublet or rented out after you buy it

Purchase price limits

There is a property purchase price limit on the home that you buy. The limit is different for each region. These limits are shown below:

How it works

With an equity loan:

  • you need at least a 5% deposit
  • the government will lend you up to 20% (up to 40% in London)
  • need a mortgage of up to 75% for the rest (up to 55% in London)

You must buy your home from a registered Help to Buy homebuilder - your agent should have a list.

There are different rules for equity loans in Wales. There are also different rules for equity loans in Scotland.

What you need

For joint applications, proof of identification will be required for each applicant.

  • Proof of ID (passport, driving license)
  • New build property details
  • Monthly income and outgoings
  • Solicitor details
